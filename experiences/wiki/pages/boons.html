Boons

Boons act as a passive version of Items, where instead of needing to be triggered manually by the player they have different, more subtle ways of activating. They also aren't lost upon being activated, unlike items.

Common Boons

Common boons can be gained by the Player through the Moon Altar event.

Boon of Casting

When you using an Active ability, deal 1 direct damage.
Boon of Knowledge

When looking inside your deck, draw the top card of your deck once you're done.
Boon of the Arcane

When you would draw from your side deck, if you have 2 unique mox colors, you may search from it instead.
Boon of the Stones

You may sacrifice Terrain cards.
Boon of Conflict

When a card you control survives an otherwise fatal blow, it strikes its opposing card immediately.
Boon of Fertility

When a card that you didn't draw is added to your hand, it gains 1 power.
Boon of the Thunder

At the start of the battle, gain 1 more energy cell.
Boon of Transformation

Cards that change form do so during your End step instead of your Draw step.
Boon of the Cheater

You can see the hand of your opponent.
Boon of the Fire

When a card perishes due to Burning, cards around it gain Burning.
Boon of Glory

When you win a battle, gain 3 extra foils.
Boon of Spells

Active abilities cost nothing, but can only be triggered once per turn.
Boon of Frost

When a sigil gets added to a card you control, place a Freeze mark on a card of your choice.
Boon of Greed

When a card you control is killed in combat, gain 1 foil.
Boon of Rituals

When playing a card, you may decrease its health to lower its cost.

(Bones and energy are worth 1 health, blood and gems are worth 2 health.)
Boon of Invocations

When a card is added to your field without being played, draw a card from your deck.
Boon of the Hoarder

You may carry 1 more item.
Boon of Protection

When a card you control loses an Armor mark, it strikes the card that broke it.
Boon of Light

Replace a side deck card from your starting hand with a Prism Mox.
Boon of Ralliment

The first opposing card you kill is added to your hand.
Boon of Replication

When playing a card with a sigil that's already on a card you control, that sigil immediately triggers once.
Boon of the Scavenger

When an opposing card dies outside of your battle step, gain 1 bone.
Boon of the Hand

At the start of the battle, you may draw 1 more card from your deck or side deck.
Boon of the Uncertain

When a card you control moves, swap its health and power.
Boon of the Swift

When a card you control moves, gain 1 bone.
Boon of Thorns

When an opposing card damages you directly, it takes 1 damage in return.
Boon of Family

If you control 2 cards that share a tribe, they gain 1 power.
Boon of Vengeance

When you play a main deck card, it gains 1 health and power per copy of itself in your discard.
Boon of the Lord

At the start of the battle, gain 2 bones.
Boon of the Weakened

When you play a card with a Negative sigil, lower the power of an opposing card by 1.

Major Boons

Major boons can be gained by the Player through the Tinkered Moon Altar event.

Boon of the Ambidextrous

When all 3 mox colors are provided on your field, you may draw twice during your draw step.
Boon of Power

At the start of the battle, the highest costing card of your most prominent resource is added to your hand.
Boon of the Candle

When you lose a battle, instead of blowing out a candle, destroy this boon.
Boon of Recycling

If a card has a copy of itself in the discard, its cost is halved, rounded down.
Boon of the Impatient

Before your draw step, you may skip your turn to gain 1 energy cell.
Boon of Time

Your opponent skips their first turn.
Boon of Synergy

If your field is full of cards that cost the same resource, they all have 1 more power and health.
Boon of Alignment

When another boon triggers, it triggers again.
Boon of the Scales

When the scales reach your candle, you cannot take any more direct damage until your next turn.
Boon of Cinders

When a card you control is killed in combat, it goes back to your hand with 1 less power and health instead of perishing.
